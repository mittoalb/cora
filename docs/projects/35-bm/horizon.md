# Horizon

*What completing the 35-BM pilot enables.*

Generality is earned customer by customer. Two next steps exercise the recipe ladder against real second and third deployments before any abstraction locks.

## APS imaging peers

Same Site as 35-BM: the equipment BC inherits the Asset hierarchy and trust topology. Differences are in Methods and sample classes. Each becomes its own project.

| Beamline | Speciality | What it adds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2-BM | 3D micro imaging, mosaic, laminography | Mature streaming reconstruction, multi-modal scans |
| 7-BM | Fuel spray imaging | Time-resolved high-pressure environment, different sample handling |
| 32-ID | 3D nano + 2D high-speed (MHz) | Dual-detector ultra-high-speed, FZP-based nano-CT |

## MAX IV

MAX IV (Lund, Sweden) is the likeliest first cross-facility deployment.

- **NanoMAX**: hard X-ray nano-imaging
- **ForMAX**: full-field tomography for biological materials and soft matter
- **MicroMAX**: macromolecular crystallography (shared trust patterns, less direct overlap)

What MAX IV proves: a Method named at 35-BM binds to a Practice at MAX IV without modifying the Method. Practice absorbs brand differences. Methods stay portable. When concrete, gets its own project doc under the same template.

## Beyond

Facility-neutral operations across photon sources, neutron sources, FELs, HPC centres. Same mechanism: each facility becomes a project doc, Methods reused, Practices freshly bound. Whether the ambition holds depends on MAX IV succeeding without straining the abstractions.
